Filter care, end to end.

Exchange, restoration, on-site soak tanks and replacement supply — the filters your exhaust system depends on, managed as one program and recorded like the assets they are.

Why it matters

A choked filter is a fire load, and an energy cost.

Filters are the exhaust system's first line of defence. Grease-laden, they restrict airflow, raise fire risk and make everything upstream work harder — drawing more power and shortening the life of the fan and duct they're meant to protect.

Most venues treat filters as an afterthought scrubbed in a sink. Summit treats them as a program: exchanged on cycle, restored in a heated non-caustic soak, verified for airflow, supplied when they're past saving — and every set recorded, so the kitchen never runs behind its filters.

01  The core · managed exchange

Exchanged, not
just cleaned.

Managed filter exchange

A restored set goes in as your soiled set comes out — collected, restored off-site and returned on a cycle matched to your cooking volume. The line never waits on a filter.

Heated non-caustic restoration

A heated bath lifts baked-on grease without caustic chemistry — effective on the deposit, gentle on the mesh and the metal, so restored filters keep their working life.

Airflow verified & recorded

Every set is checked after restoration and the condition photographed — performance recovered and documented to the filter register, not assumed.

Baffle & honeycomb, any canopy

Both filter types, whatever configuration your canopy runs — single venue or a swap-stock pool sized across a whole group.

03  On-site soak tanks

Want it in-house?
We'll set you up.

For high-volume kitchens that soak nightly, an on-site heated tank can be the right call — supplied by Summit and kept working as part of the same program, not dropped off and forgotten.

Purchase or rental

Own it outright or rent it — structured to suit the site and scoped at assessment, with installation and commissioning handled.

Chemicals on cycle

Non-caustic soak solution replenished on a schedule we carry — the tank never sits flat because someone forgot to reorder.

Tank service & upkeep

Periodic servicing, heater and safety checks, and waste-handling guidance — the tank stays an asset, not a liability in the corner.

Staff induction & safe use

Your kitchen team inducted on safe operation and handling — documented, so the tank helps your WHS position instead of complicating it.

04  Supply & the register

Replaced before failure,
recorded for life.

Filters are consumables — mesh fatigues, frames corrode, baffles bend. Because every set passes through our hands on cycle, wear gets caught early: replacements are sized, supplied and fitted before a failed filter becomes a compliance gap.

01New baffle and honeycomb filters supplied, sized to your canopy.
02Damaged and corroded sets flagged at inspection, replaced on the next cycle.
03Every set tracked on a per-site filter register — condition, airflow, history.
04The register feeds the same evidence pack as your exhaust compliance.
