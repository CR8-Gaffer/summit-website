Reporting

The evidence pack.

A clean you can't prove is a clean you don't have. Every Summit service closes with the same deliverable — photographed, measured, certified evidence that stands up to your insurer, your auditor and your fire-safety assessment.

01  Every visit, every site

Four things leave
with the crew.

Before & after photography

Every system component photographed in its found condition and its finished condition — the state of the asset, evidenced, not asserted.

Measured readings

Grease-deposit readings taken at test points before and after the service and recorded against benchmark — numbers, not adjectives.

Limitations declared

Anything that couldn't be accessed is recorded, not glossed — with the reason and the remedy (like an access panel) proposed. An honest record protects you; a silent one doesn't.

Next service scheduled

The record closes with the next due date already set from your usage profile — the obligation never floats.

component.

03  The certificate

Named to the standard,
or it isn't evidence.

A generic “certificate of cleaning” is often not accepted at face value by fire-safety assessors and insurers. A valid certificate names AS 1851-2012, states what was serviced and to what result, declares its limitations, and sets the next due date. That's what we issue — every service, without being asked.

04  Where it goes

Built for the three people
who'll ask for it.

YOUR INSURER

When the claim comes

After a kitchen fire, maintenance records are among the first documents requested. A dated, standard-named evidence chain is often the difference between a claim that settles smoothly and one that gets contested.

YOUR AFSS · NSW

When the statement is due

Where a building's fire safety schedule lists the kitchen exhaust system, the NSW Annual Fire Safety Statement relies on an accredited practitioner's assessment — and that assessment relies on maintenance evidence. Ours is built to hand straight over.

YOUR AUDITOR

When the audit lands

Council, food-safety and internal audits all ask the same question: can you produce the records? Consolidated, current and per-site — yes, in minutes.

Wondering what the schedule itself should look like? See the compliance guide for indicative service frequencies under AS 1851-2012 as applied to usage.

05  The asset register

Scan it.
Know its story.

Every serviceable asset we touch carries a QR tag tied to your site's register — so the record isn't just filed somewhere, it's findable from the asset itself.

QR-tagged assets

Filter sets, access panels, soak tanks and key components tagged at first service — the tag is the asset's identity in your register.

Scanned every service

Crews scan on site, and the visit, readings and photos log against that asset — history builds itself, with nothing left to transcription.

History on demand

Scan a tag or just ask — the component's full service history, condition photos and next-due date, produced in minutes.

One register per site

The register sits behind the evidence pack — the same records, organised by asset, rolled up portfolio-wide for multi-site groups.
